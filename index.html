How to play Twenty–Nine

A 4-player partnership trick-taking game. You and the player opposite you are a team. Bid what you think your team can score, then win tricks to prove it.

1 · The cards

32 cards — 7 up to Ace. Jacks and Nines are the strongest, not Aces. Strongest on the left:

Only four ranks are worth points. Everything else is worth zero:

28 points live in the cards, and the last trick is worth 1 more — 29 in total. That's the name.

2 · The deal

Everyone gets 4 cards. You bid on those alone — then everyone gets 4 more, for 8 each. Partners sit opposite: you and the player across the table are one team.

3 · Bidding

Starting left of the dealer, each player either bids higher or passes. Bids run from 16 to 28. Once you pass you're out of the auction for that hand.

The highest bidder is promising their team will capture at least that many points this hand. Bid too high and you hand the round to your opponents.

4 · Trump — the twist

The winning bidder secretly picks a trump suit from their own cards. Nobody else knows it, and it does nothing until it is revealed.

When you can't follow the suit that was led, you may ask for trump to be revealed. From then on it's face-up for everyone, and trump beats any normal card. Careful: if you're the one who revealed it, you must play a trump that turn if you hold one.

Revealing early gives information away. Revealing late can lose you tricks. That timing is the heart of the game.

5 · Playing a trick

The lead player puts down any card. Everyone else must follow that suit if they can. If you can't, play anything.

Highest card of the led suit wins — unless someone trumps it once trump is revealed, in which case the highest trump wins. The winner leads next. Eight tricks per hand.

6 · Scoring

Add up the points your team captured, plus 1 if you took the last trick.

  • Bidding team reaches its bid → they win the hand.
  • Bidding team falls short → they're set, and the defenders win it.

The side that wins the hand takes 1 match point. First team to 6 match points wins the match.

Quick tips

  • Count the Jacks and Nines — they're 5 of the 29 points on their own.
  • Your partner's win is your win. Throw point cards onto tricks they're winning.
  • Don't waste high trumps on worthless tricks.
  • If an opponent shows they're out of a suit, avoid leading it — they'll trump.
